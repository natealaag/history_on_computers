A Brief Summary of His Invention

Sir Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web in 1989 while working at CERN. His goal was to facilitate information sharing among researchers. He developed the first web browser and server, along with key technologies like HTML (HyperText Markup Language) and HTTP (HyperText Transfer Protocol). This innovation transformed how we access and share information, laying the foundation for the modern internet.

The Importance of his invention

The world wide web opened up the internet to everyone, not just scientists. It connected the world in a way that made it much easier for people to get information, share, and communicate. It has since allowed people to share their work and thoughts through social networking sites, blogs, video sharing, and more.
