Sir Tim Berners-Lee And His Contributions to Computer Science

About Sir Tim Berners-Lee

Sir Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist who invented the World Wide Web in 1989. His creation changed how people connect and share information across the world. He also developed the first web browser and website, making the internet easier to use. Today, he is working on a project called "Solid," which helps people have more control over their personal information online.

Early life

His parents were mathematicians and worked on the first commercial computer at the University of Manchester. As a child, Berners-Lee was fascinated by computers. He also enjoyed mathematics and electronics. For schooling he first attended Sheen Mount primary school. Then he went on to Emanuel School in London, from 1969 to 1973. After that, he studied at Queen's College, Oxford, from 1973 to 1976. When he entered Queen's College at Oxford University in 1972, Berners-Lee chose to major in Physics, hoping to utilize his native talents in both scientific theory and practical application. While at Oxford, Berners-Lee built his first computer.
